The Ledger Private Finance

Privacy Policy

Effective 22 July 2026 Grumpy Guy Labs LLC

The Ledger is a manual money tracker. You type your own numbers in. It does not connect to your bank, it has no ads, and there is no version of this business where your financial data gets sold. There is nothing to sell.

This policy explains what we hold, why we hold it, and how to get it back or get rid of it.

01Who we are

The Ledger is made by Grumpy Guy Labs LLC, a Maryland limited liability company. When this policy says "we" or "us," that is who it means. When it says "you," it means the person using the app.

Questions about anything here go to theledgersupport@gmail.com.

02What we collect

Your account

An email address and a password. Passwords are hashed by our authentication provider and stored in a form we cannot read. We never see your password, and we cannot recover it for you.

The numbers you enter

Everything you type into The Ledger: accounts and balances, transactions, plans, deductions, business figures, and any names or notes you choose to attach to them. This is your ledger. We store it so it can sync to your devices and, on Pro, so it can be restored from a backup.

We collect this because you entered it. We do not derive it, buy it, or pull it from anywhere else.

Subscription status

Whether you have an active subscription, which plan, when it renews or expires, and the identifiers our billing provider uses to tell us. This is what unlocks Pro features.

Feedback you send

If you use the in-app feedback form, we store what you wrote so we can read it and act on it.

Ordinary technical data

Our hosting and database providers keep standard server logs, which can include IP address, browser type, and timestamps. This is the routine exhaust of running a website. We use it to keep the service up and to investigate problems.

03What we do not do

  • No bank connections. The Ledger does not link to your bank, does not use Plaid or any similar aggregator, and never asks for your banking credentials. It cannot see your accounts, because it is not connected to them.
  • No selling or renting your data. Not to advertisers, not to data brokers, not to anyone. We do not sell or share personal information as those terms are used under U.S. state privacy laws.
  • No advertising and no ad trackers. There are no ad networks, no advertising identifiers, and no third-party marketing pixels in the app.
  • No profiling your finances. We do not analyze your ledger to build a profile of you, and we do not use your financial entries to train anything.
  • No card numbers. Payment details never touch our servers. See section 05.

04How we use what we collect

Narrowly, and only to run the product:

  • To show you your own data and sync it across your devices.
  • To create and restore backups, if you are on Pro.
  • To know whether your subscription is active, so the right features are unlocked.
  • To send you transactional email about your subscription — a purchase confirmation, an upcoming renewal, a billing problem.
  • To answer you when you contact support or send feedback.
  • To keep the service running, secure, and debuggable.

That is the complete list. If we ever want to use your data for something outside it, we will ask first.

05Payments

Subscriptions are handled by RevenueCat, which uses Stripe to process payments. When you subscribe, your card details go directly to Stripe. We never receive them, never see them, and have nothing to store.

What we do receive is your subscription state: active or not, which plan, and when it expires. RevenueCat holds your billing email and subscription history under its own privacy policy, and Stripe holds the payment record under its own.

Charges appear on your card statement as GRUMPY GUY LABS.

06Who else touches your data

The Ledger is run by one person, on a small stack. These are the providers involved, and all of them are used as infrastructure — none of them are given your data to use for their own purposes:

  • Convex — the database and sync backend. Your ledger lives here.
  • Vercel — hosting for the app and the website.
  • RevenueCat — subscription management, and the sender of subscription email.
  • Stripe — payment processing.

We will also disclose data if the law genuinely requires it — a valid subpoena, court order, or equivalent legal process — or where necessary to protect someone's safety. If Grumpy Guy Labs is ever sold or merged, your data may transfer as part of that business, and this policy travels with it.

07Market data

The Markets view fetches public economic and market figures — consumer price data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, interest rates from the New York Fed, and index and sector returns from a public market data source.

Those requests are made by our own server, and they carry nothing about you. We ask those sources for numbers. We do not tell them anything.

08Your data is yours

This is not a slogan we intend to weasel out of:

  • Export. You can export your data from inside the app, at any time, on any plan, including the free one. It is not held hostage behind a subscription.
  • Access and correct. Your data is visible and editable in the app. Everything we hold about your ledger is something you can already see.
  • Delete. You can delete your account. Email theledgersupport@gmail.com and we will remove your account and its data.

Depending on where you live, you may have additional statutory rights over your personal information. Rather than gate those behind a legal process, we extend the same handling to everyone: ask us, and we will export it, correct it, or delete it. We will not charge you for it or ask why.

09How long we keep it

Your ledger stays until you delete it or delete your account. Backups are retained on a rolling basis so older restore points exist, and they are removed when the account is deleted.

After account deletion, residual copies may persist briefly in routine provider backups before aging out. Some billing records are kept by Stripe as long as tax and financial recordkeeping law requires, which is outside our control.

10Security

Data is encrypted in transit. Passwords are hashed. Access to the production database is limited to the operator of the business. Backups are scoped to the account that made them.

No service can promise perfect security, and we will not pretend otherwise. What we can say is that the smaller the pile of data, the smaller the target — which is part of why The Ledger never asks for bank credentials in the first place.

11Children

The Ledger is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. If you believe a child has created an account, email us and we will delete it.

12Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the effective date at the top changes with it. If a change materially affects how your data is handled, we will tell you in the app or by email before it takes effect — not by quietly editing this page.
